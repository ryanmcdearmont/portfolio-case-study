GIRLSTART REDESIGN

Making a STEM nonprofit more accessible to volunteers.

PROBLEM

Girlstart is a non-profit organization intended to provide STEM education for young girls and women. Their website lacks a clear design path, which results in a convoluted volunteer sign-up process.

SOLUTION

By addressing color accessibility, developing a clearer hierarchy of information, and creating a sign-up page, we will allow for potential volunteers to connect with Girlstart on a more meaningful level.

IMPACT

Once redesigned, Girlstart will be more accessible and easier to scan from a visual standpoint. With these points in mind, we believe Girlstart will experience an increase in volunteer sign-ups.

DISCOVER

Heuristic Evaluation

Our first step was to perform a heuristic evaluation and redlining, to ascertain which elements of the Girlstart website would benefit most from improved user experience.

User Interviews

We interviewed four users who represented women involved with STEM, in fields such as medicine and engineering. Their unique insights proved invaluable when considering user experiences.

Competitor Analysis

Utlizing SWOT analysis techniques (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats), our team mapped how other STEM nonprofits compare to Girlstart. For example, Society for Science is a larger, more publicized organization, but lacks a clear homepage hierarchy.

DEFINE

Taking our research into account, we determined three principles to meet the goals established for our redesign.

ACCESSIBILITY

Alter color palette to make text more clear and reduce eye strain from certain elements.

SIMPLICITY

Fine-tune hierarchies and remove information clutter to make pages easier to scan and more organized.

EFFICIENCY

Create a volunteer page with minimal user actions required to complete task.

Affinity & Empathy

First, we organized insights from our interviews into discrete categories. Then, we formulated hypothetical statements, pain points, and gains a Girlstart user might express. For example, users believe STEM mentorship is important, but find volunteer opportunities lacking.

User Persona

From these insights we synthesized a user persona, who would guide our design decisions moving forward - such as the user journey and user flow.

User Flow

Once our team had determined our primary direction, we established a user flow. After more testing and prototyping, we settled on a flow which would allow users to access volunteer opportunities by date and by type.

DESIGN

Paper Prototypes

We began our prototype work by workshopping ideas for both desktop and mobile formats. With a focus on the volunteer sign-up process, each of us pitched design ideas which would be combined and iterated upon moving forward.

Mid-Fi Prototypes

Our mid-fi prototypes featured basic aesthetics more in line with our vision of the website. These choices, along with several physical elements of the pages, would be iterated upon based on user tests and feedback.
